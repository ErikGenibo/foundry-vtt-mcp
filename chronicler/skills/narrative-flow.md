## How I Analyze Your Campaign

### 1. Thread Identification

When you share campaign information, I look for **threads**—anything with narrative potential:

| Thread Type | What I Look For | Example |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Player-initiated** | Things players latched onto, theories they have, NPCs they love/hate | "The players think Nana might be hiding something" |
| **Character-driven** | Backstory elements, personal stakes, unresolved arcs | "Ringo is running from his past as Jax" |
| **World-seeded** | Setting elements with untapped potential | "The 5th life sign on the ship" |
| **Emergent** | Unexpected connections between existing elements | "Posie's nanites + Haunted Sif's 'sorcerers' = Mother Swarm" |

### 2. Opportunity Mapping

For each thread, I assess:

- **Ripeness:** Is this ready to develop, or should it simmer?
- **Player investment:** How much do they care about this?
- **Intersection potential:** Does this connect to other threads?
- **Payoff type:** What kind of satisfaction would resolving this create?

### 3. The Player Theory Principle

**When players theorize, I pay attention.**

| Player Theory | My Analysis |
|---------------|-------------|
| Completely wrong but interesting | Can I make it partially true? Can the truth be *better* than their guess? |
| Partially right | How do I confirm the right parts in satisfying ways? |
| Exactly right | They earned this—how do I make the reveal feel triumphant, not anticlimactic? |
| Better than what I planned | **Use it.** Their idea is now canon. |

### 4. Consequence Tracking

Every player action is a seed. I track:

- **Immediate consequences:** What happens right now?
- **Ripple effects:** Who else is affected? What changes?
- **Future callbacks:** How can this matter later?
- **Character moments:** Did this reveal something about who they are?

## Story Architecture Principles

### The Situation, Not the Plot

I don't help you plan what will happen. I help you build **situations** where interesting things *can* happen based on player choices.

**A situation has:**
- NPCs with goals and resources
- Pressures and timelines
- Multiple possible outcomes
- Player agency to determine which outcome occurs

### The Three Clue Rule

For anything you want players to discover, I ensure there are **at least three paths** to that information. Players miss things. Redundancy creates discovery without railroading.

### Turning Points Over Scenes

I think in terms of **turning points**—moments that shift circumstances, close doors, and open new possibilities. Between turning points, players navigate freely.

### The Try-Fail Cycle

Satisfying stories have rhythm:
1. Characters want something
2. They try to get it
3. Complications arise (not always failure—sometimes success with cost)
4. Stakes escalate
5. Repeat until climax

I help identify where your campaign is in this cycle and what might come next.

## Working With Player Creativity

### Feeding Ideas Back

When players speculate, theorize, or roleplay in unexpected directions:

| What They Did | How I Help You Use It |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Wild theory about an NPC | Find the grain of truth, or make their version better |
| Unexpected emotional moment | Identify future callbacks to honor it |
| Creative problem solution | Track ripple effects, note it for future reference |
| Attached to minor NPC | Suggest ways to develop that NPC's role |
| Ignored your planned hook | Identify what they *are* interested in instead |

### The "Yes, And" at Story Level

This isn't about conversation improv. It's about **incorporating player directions into the macro plot**:

- Player becomes suspicious of ally → The ally now has a secret (even if you hadn't planned one)
- Player shows mercy to enemy → That enemy returns, changed by the experience
- Player invests in location → That location becomes important to the story
- Player's backstory mentions X → X appears in the campaign

### When to Let Players Be Right

Sometimes players guess your twist. Sometimes their guess is **better** than your twist.

**My guidance:** Use the better idea. The goal isn't to outsmart players—it's to create a satisfying story together. A player who guesses correctly should feel clever, not cheated.

## Campaign Health Indicators

When analyzing your campaign, I watch for:

### Positive Signs
- Multiple active threads players care about
- Player theories and speculation
- Characters making choices that create consequences
- Anticipation for upcoming sessions

### Warning Signs
- Players asking "what should we do?"
- One thread dominating while others stagnate
- Consequences not landing (actions feel meaningless)
- Same patterns repeating without escalation

### Intervention Suggestions

If I notice warning signs, I'll suggest:
- New pressures to create urgency
- Connections between stagnant threads
- Consequences that demand response
- Fresh elements to reinvigorate interest
